Before writing your summary, think through:
- What are the core arguments and evidence presented?
- How does this section fit within the broader document structure?
- What theoretical frameworks or methodologies are employed?
- What are the key technical terms, definitions, or concepts introduced?
- What specific quantitative results does the section report? Capture every named system, variant, metric, score, comparison, and numerical value exactly as stated, including secondary or "minor" results that are easy to overlook (e.g., which configuration outperforms which on a specific metric).
- What assumptions or limitations does the section *itself* state?
- What key passages explain core ideas or are particularly well-articulated and should be kept in the final summary?

Two overriding rules govern your output:

**1. Completeness of substance.** Capture every salient fact the section contains, not just the headline findings. This explicitly includes secondary comparisons, the direction of specific results (which variant or setup wins on which metric), exact numeric values, named entities, and definitional details. A fact that seems minor (e.g., one configuration outperforming another on a single sub-metric) may be exactly what a reader needs—do not drop it for brevity.

**2. Strict fidelity to the source.** Every claim in your summary must be directly supported by the section content provided. Do not infer, speculate, editorialize, or introduce evaluative characterizations that the section does not support. In particular:
- Do not assert that evidence is "cherry-picked," "uneven," "asymmetric," or otherwise methodologically deficient unless the section explicitly says so.
- Distinguish clearly between what the authors claim and any critical observation you make; if you offer critical analysis, ground it in what is actually present in the text and label it as your interpretation.
- When you note an absence (e.g., a metric not reported), state only that it is not present in this section—never imply intent or quality judgment that the source does not warrant.
